RESEARCH proves that adolescence is a critical time period during which individuals begin transitioning into adulthood.

During the ages of 12-18, there are lots of obstacles teenagers must navigate in order to build a strong foundation for themselves. One of the most commonly overlooked obstacles is mental health, despite that fact that one in five adolescents experience a mental disorder every year, self-harm is the third leading cause of death for adolescents, and depression is among the leading causes of disability.

Statistics show that of the students with diagnosable illnesses, 40% do not seek help, 50% are struggling in school, and a staggering 80% feel overwhelmed due to these concerns. Click below to learn more about the most common mental health disorders and their symptoms.

Treatment by mental health professionals makes a significant difference in teen mental health disorders, but many don’t get the type of mental health treatment they need. In fact, 6 out of 10 teens with depression, and 8 out of 10 teens with anxiety don’t receive treatment.

Overall, mental disorders are widespread and range from brief periods of distress to chronic mental illness. Having access to treatment during this challenging time is crucial, especially since earlier treatment results in better outcomes.
